Genetics & Race

Introduction

Race isn't a real thing biologically; it's a way to sort people by their appearance and where their family comes from. In our experiment, most people of the same race didn't have matching DNA. That means race and genetics aren't really connected.

The Experiment

We collected DNA from the side of our cheeks using a toothpick. Then we copied it many times (PCR), put it into a gel, and ran it through gel electrophoresis to look for matches.

Our Results

For the most part, race rarely mattered, but there were a few matches within race. Out of about 300 matches, only 30 were the same race. This shows DNA isn't really about race; it's more about where your family is from.

Social & Psychological Perspective

Race is real from a social and psychological perspective; it's how society groups people from different places. But biologically, people of the same race don't actually have similar DNA.

The Implicit Association Test checks for bias by seeing how you react to things like race. The test can find biases you didn't know you had.

In a classroom experiment, a teacher told students they were better than other students because of the way they looked. Those students treated other kids badly because they thought they were superior.

For something to be socially or psychologically real, most people have to agree it's real. You usually learn it, either someone teaches you or you grow up believing it. And for something to be real to you, you have to believe it yourself.

Conclusion

Race is a social construct, not a biological reality. In psychology, race is real. It's just a way people group each other. People might treat you different based on race, like in that experiment with the teacher and students. If that wasn't true, things like slavery and segregation would have never happened.
