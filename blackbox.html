Black Box Investigation

Project Overview

We were challenged with creating a replica of a black box made by our teacher. This box makes it so whenever you pour water on the top funnel, either less water, more water, or the same amount of water pours out the bottom.

How It Works

The black box has 2 different tubes where they branch out. One goes to the end of the box and pours out. The second one gets poured into a cup on a piece of cardboard. That piece of cardboard is being weighed down by some rocks in a cup on the other side. When enough water gets poured into the cup, it overweighs the rocks which causes it to tilt and pour into the funnel.

Possible Outcomes

  • Less Water
  • More Water
  • Same Amount

Test Results

Test Water In (mL) Water Out (mL) Result
1 1000 390 Less
2 1000 1700 More
3 1000 960 Same
4 1000 1000 Same

Overall, the results don't show a major trend. There are 3 outcomes: Less water comes out, More water comes out, or The same amount of water pours out.

Building the Replica

We built it piece by piece. First, we found the optimal cardboard box, then we drilled holes in the top to create the funnel opening. We cut the tubes to fit in the correct ways. Then we made the cups to actually hold and carry the water. Lastly, we had to make the up and down mechanism for the rocks and the water. This was quite hard but we made it work.

We had to do a couple of trials before water actually came out because we didn't account for where it would exit from. After we fixed that problem, we had a constant supply of water varying from little to more than we started with. It wasn't constant which was bad, but we were on a time crunch and it was the best we could do.
