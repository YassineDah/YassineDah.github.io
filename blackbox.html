In my new media & tech class at ESUMS we were challenged with creating a replica of a black box made by our teacher. This box makes it so whenever you pour water on the top funnel either less water more water or the same amount of water pours out the bottom, I think that i made a good replica of the black box.

Claim & Evidence

The black box has 2 different tubes in where they branch out, one goes to the end of the box and pours out, the second one gets poured into a up on apiece of cardboard, that piece of cardboard is being weighed down by some rocks in a cup on the other side of the cardboard, enough water gets poured into the cup it overweighs the rocks which causes it to tilt and pour into the funnel. In our teachers black box me and my team tested it to see what were some of the results here is what we came up with, We poured 1000ML of water each trial usually and for the first 4 tests, 1000 went in and 390 came out then another 100 went in and 1700 came out soon after another 1000 went in and around 960 came out, Lastly we poured in 1000 once again and 1000 came out. Overall the results don't show a major trend, all there is to note is that there are 3 outcomes. Less water comes out, More water comes out, The same amount of water pours out. When we tried designing out prototypes we tried to keep this in mind and create a design that copies this theme. We built it piece by piece, first we found the optimal cardboard box then we decided to drill holes in the top to create the funnel opening then we cut the tubes to fit in the correct ways. Then we made the cups to actually hold and carry the water. Lastly we had to make the up and down mechanism for the rocks and the water. This was quite hard but we brute forced it and made it work. Our data we got from building the replica were a bit iffy, we had to do a couple of trials before water actually came out because we didn't account to where it would exit from, after we fixed that problem we had a constant supply of water varying of little water to more water then we originally started with. But it wasn't constant which was bad but we were on a time crunch and it was the best thing we could have done.
